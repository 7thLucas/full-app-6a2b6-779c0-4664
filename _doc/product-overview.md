# Product Overview — Competitor Monitoring App

> Working title: **PulseWatch** (provisional — to be replaced when the owner names the app).
> Status: early scoping. Some facts below are still being confirmed with the owner and are
> marked **(to confirm)**. Update this file as those answers land.

## What it is

A competitor monitoring app: a single place where a business watches its rivals and finds
out about meaningful moves — a price drop, a new feature, a fresh promotion — **before a
customer brings it up first**. It turns scattered, manual competitor-checking into one
calm, organized feed with alerts.

The product's core promise is **signal over noise**: not a firehose of scraped data, but
the handful of changes that actually matter, surfaced early enough to react.

## The problem it solves

Teams get blindsided. A competitor quietly cuts prices or ships a feature, and the business
only learns about it when a prospect throws it into a sales call or a customer asks why
they're paying more. Today this monitoring is manual, sporadic, and lives in someone's
browser tabs and memory — so changes are caught late or missed entirely.

## Who it's for

An **internal tool for QuantumByte's own team** — the company's in-house competitive-
intelligence cockpit, watching rival AI app-building platforms. This is **not a
customer-facing product**; it's used in-house.

- **Primary: product & growth** at QuantumByte — staying ahead of competing platforms'
  pricing, features, and positioning.
- **Marketing** — tracking rivals' campaigns, messaging, and promotions.
- **Leadership** — wanting the headline on competitive moves without daily manual checks.

## What it monitors

Three change categories (the owner is confirming which matter most):
1. **Pricing** — price changes, new plans/tiers, discounts and promotions.
2. **Product** — new features, releases, and notable changes.
3. **Marketing** — campaigns, messaging shifts, and announcements.

## Core workflow (MVP)

1. Add the competitors to watch.
2. The app tracks each competitor across the chosen change categories.
3. Detected changes land in a single chronological feed (a change log / timeline).
4. The owner gets **alerts** on meaningful changes so nothing is caught late.
5. Each change is reviewed/acknowledged by the owner — the core verified operation.

## The verified operation

The domain event this app exists to perform: **a competitor change reviewed and
acknowledged** by the owner (a real change detected, surfaced, and actioned). Not page
views, not logins — a change actually triaged.

## Positioning & strategic principles

- **Early, not exhaustive.** Being first to know beats knowing everything. Bias toward
  timely alerts on what matters.
- **Signal over noise.** Curate the few changes worth acting on; suppress the rest.
- **One pane of glass.** Replace scattered manual checks with a single organized feed.
- **Owner-first.** Built for a busy decision-maker who wants the headline and the "so what,"
  not raw data.
